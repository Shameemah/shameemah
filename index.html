Biography

A life spent trying to reconcile both the creative and the analytical, finding purpose and going unafraid.

  • For many years in high school, I found myself constantly torn between identifying as an artist or a programmer. I was made to believe that it was impossible to be both creative and analytical, so my younger self felt the need to pick one and neglect the other. This became especially overwhelming for me when I had to pick a major for college.

  • I was never quite sure what I wanted to major in for college, but architecture made sense to me because it allowed me to be creative and technical. However,I quickly learned in my first year of college that architecture did not allow me to be creative in the way I had imagined. I had to make the difficult decision to switch majors after being enrolled as an architecture student for a year.

  • Ultimately, I decided to switch majors to Information Technology and Management because I had always loved programming, and was excited at the thought of using technology to solve basic problems that people in my home country, Ghana, face on the daily.

  • In the last four years, I have discovered that IT has given me the opportunity to combine my creative and analytical sides. College has been a long ride of rediscovering myself and unlearning the things that made me feel like I could only be one thing or the other, but not both. I am currently in my final semester of college, and am excited to start work as a Product Manager after graduation.
